Escalation Rules

The Wasteland is not a safe place. Many-a-time will the lone traveller find themselves surrounded by many of the scum and villainy found abroad. Controlling the chaos is the whole goal of Escalation!

Escalation requires a ​Motive​, ​Verbal-Engagement​ and ​Reaction!

Escalation requires ​Motivation! ​Be it as simple as a Raider wanting the caps in a Wastelanders pocket or someone insulting your mother with something involving a mole-rat and a large sum of Fancy-Lad snack cakes, motivation for violence is commonly not hard to find in the wasteland! But do not abuse it.

Valid Motives include, but are not limited to!:

-​The Player is of an opposing Faction!

Legion and NCR forces especially fall into this category. If you’re actively and openly at war with an opposing faction. It is entirely justified to give them an ultimatum and begin Escalation!

-​Mugging and Enslavement!

While normally it is Raiders whom take part of this practice. ​Wastelanders​ are not always naturally humble people. In desperate times, anyone will try to take what is not theirs!

- Mugging/robbing/enslavement at 15 minutes.

To prevent fresh spawns from being ganked as they come into the wasteland, a grace period of 15 minutes is applicable until you can rob/mug/enslave someone! ​Camping the Spawn-Points is strictly forbidden

-​The Player has a Bounty or some form of reward attached to their head!

Says what it is on the Tin. If you’ve got a contract on a person's life, you’re more than free to begin escalation against them!

-​The Player has committed IC actions against you!

Be it a rather vulgar-insult, thievery or many other infractions that rustle even the sturdiest of hearts. The Wasteland is an unforgiving place and wild place! If you play your cards, you better be prepared to deal with the consequences!

Escalation requires ​Verbal-Engagement! ​It’s what it says on the tin! You must provide a Verbal-Threat or Ultimatum to your target in order to engage them!

Poor Verbal-Engagement ​will​cause problems! ​You don’t need to write a massive paragraph or anything. But do not simply go “SURRENDER OR DIE” in all caps anytime you Escalate, it’s simply poor form!

Pointing is not valid-escalation! ​However; If a player ​continues to just run away from you when you point them to stop, this is grounds for escalation! Read further into Reactions to know especially if they are planning on retaliating!

-Escalation requires a ​reaction ​from the party being escalated on. This reaction can be the following:

-​Pulling out a weapon!

-​Fleeing! ​Moving around sporadically (Obviously dodging) is also grounds for escalation being accepted.

-​Verbal Threat!

-​Using Pre-Combat Chems!

However, if they ​Surrender, ​you should be courteous to your fellow player. If they surrender, take your spoils of wars and be done with it! Executing hostile-factions captures is acceptable, through some factions such as the NCR prefer to take captures above simply slaughering them. If someone is attempting to negotiate or is complying with your demands, you should not assault them for trying to negotiate, but instead interact with them. If their case is bad, you may still kill them after denying them!

If you are caught in the crossfire of a fight, that is ​not ​an excuse to join

either side's conflict. You should firstly try to get out of the way! This also includes wandering into a fight! ​​You may still enter into conflict with non-faction groups, but should escalate clearly and openly! ​You should not however enter Faction-Disputes if you are not openly aligned with that faction! ​This can cause immense confusion and lead to a catastrophe for every bystander.

-Escalation after raid.

After a raid is finished, you’re expected to finish up your pillaging and exit the base quickly. Take your spoils of war, erect the flags and banners of your Faction. But do not stay in the base for longer than 10 minutes.

-Raids only viable at the 30 minute mark!

To give each faction ample time to prepare with organization, and fortifications, you will be able to raid a faction after 30 minutes! If you want to raid a faction a second time, you will have to wait a twenty minutes grace period until you can raid once more.

-Actions that aren’t able to do with escalation

Things like poisoning drinks, or food, or other actions that can’t be done without verbally telling the victim, put your faith in the staff team- and ahelp! We will determine whether your reasoning in exemption from escalation is good for roleplay or not, and provide you with an answer! If the answer is yes, you can go ahead with your desired action! If the answer is no, you have to reconsider either your roleplay, or escalate properly!

You may Escalate through radio, ​but ​the opposing side must answer to it back!​!

​If you believe the enemy group is simply ignoring your radio-calls on a Meta-Level. Feel

free to A-HELP about it! This is to avoid confusion.

-

Additionally, submitting a ticket in regards to off-screen escalation is advised, this will be delegated through means of direct narrations to inform targets of the oncoming attack.

Roleplay Rules

To ensure each and every character in the server is appropriate to the setting, there will be roleplay rules set out to strive for avoidance of LRP!

-Characters must be immersive to the Fallout setting!

A soldier of the NCR can be a humane empathetic trooper, humble- and simply only wanting their caps at the end of the day, or a scummy person who does underhand deals to his fellow comrades selling chems. Whilst a member of Caesar’s Legion may be a brutal, brainwashed, ruthless tribal, who seeks to crucify dozens by the end of this month in the name of Mars. A raider may be a sadistic psychopath, or pusher of chems, etc! Your character must make sense by Fallout standards, or they may be deemed requiring change at the reasonable behest of Kocaeli F13 staff!

-Characters must stay relatively true to their roles!

A raider should act like a raider, a scribe like a scribe, a trooper like a trooper, and a legionary like a legionary. Without much proper reason should they be acting like the other, should a trooper act like a legionary, or a scribe like a raider.

-Word Usage

Words like catatonic, matrix, gank, and mutual terms, aren’t to be used ICly! You may use filler words like, a blank look in their eye, leaving to other places, going asleep, etc, etc. Additionally, while we allow full freedom for the in-character use of Slurs, excessively using this language to the point that it simply breaks immersion is not tolerated.

-Character knowledge

-Wastelanders are usually isolated people. While word may come down from the passing Trader or the occasional odd-light or sound in the sky; most of what the Wasteland witness and see can be put down to Superstitions and the mad-ramblings of Brahmin-Shaggers.

-While a few Wastelanders have heard about the Brotherhood of Steel; they have nothing more than fables and the stories to go on. NCR are allowed to have a basic understanding of the rank-structure as they’ve had tenuous relations with the faction.

-The Enclave are a boogeyman story to most wastelanders. Some crazed prospectors may claim that the Enclave still exists shattered and rearming themselves, the triumphs of a once proud organization are mostly told in fabricated lies and stories told to the young. Extinguished by the NCR in Navarro, the NCR has extremely limited knowledge and an incredible ignorance to the continued existence of their old foe.

-Realistic hair colors and types.

Let’s be honest! Is someone with big ol’ pink hair gonna survive in the wasteland? No, they’re gonna get their hair pulled and decapitated with a machete in the harsh, harsh post-apocalyptic society that is the United States of America! A member of the staff reserves the right to tell ya if yer hair/hair color is untrue to the Fallout standard. More tame colors that deviate from the usual blond, red, black, brown, are fine in moderation! But big flowing hair that isn’t witnessed in Fallout isn’t. ​Use common sense!

-Know the setting.

The year is 2277, before The First Battle of Hoover Dam. The events before being the Destruction of Hopeville and Ashton, it is the starting of the conflict between the Bull and the Bear!

Synthetics ​have not reached the West-Coast territories, as such; such characters are banned from use.

Characters from the ​East-Coast​ require admin-clearance.

Captures and You! ​(A Guide)

-You are ​NOT ​required to take Captures as any faction. The only faction that is strongly suggested to do so is the New California Republic.

Caesar: ​The Self-Proclaimed “Son of Mars”, subjugator of eighty seven tribes and the charismatic De-Facto leader of The Legion. His word is law and his will the fundamental strength of The Legion.

Legate: ​The Legion’s Field-Marshal. These handpicked, exceptional men answer only to Caesar himself and are absolutely the fiercest fighters in the Legion. This role is not present within normal gameplay.

Praetorian​: Praetorians are the personal security detail of Caesar. They are immensely skilled unarmed combatants wielding often the finest unarmed weapons the Legion has at their disposal. They are fearless and will utterly give their lives in defence of Caesar. Not seen within Kocaeli F13.

Centurion​: Field Commanders and Draconian in discipline. Centurion’s are the terrifying and battle-hardened warriors of The Legion. Equipped with the finest weapons and armor and

commanding with an iron-fist, Centurions are both feared and respected by the men serving underneath them. Centurion is the highest ranked member of The Legion during normal play! Centurions are typically allowed without escalation to beat the heck out of soldiers who disobey his orders with proper reasoning ICly, and even as far as to kill them. This specific job exemption from escalating on their own faction is one of a kind! But don’t take this as a certification to kill people out of random, you can be ahelped should your reasoning be null!

Decanus​: Displaying leadership skills after countless battles. Decanus are equivalent to the officers of the New California Republic. Helping command and ensure discipline amongst the Legionnaires beneath them. In-Game, Decanus lead through brutality and are equipped with a versatile set of weapons to help them be a dominating combat-threat

Libitors​: Specialist Decanus who are armed with the powerful but ancient M1919 light-machine gun. They provide a critical support role and when used properly are one of the most powerful troops The Legion can field! They are however extremely slow, limited in number and have sub-par protection to their much more CQC oriented brothers, The Decanus.

Explorers:​Fast as the wind and armed with long-rifles. Explorers fill a niche role of being excellent scouts and long-range harassers for The Legion ingame! They are slightly above rank than Veteran Legionaries. They are a great support role that can drastically assist in dealing with the NCR’s prevalent snipers!

Legionairy: ​The common soldier of the Legion. Armed with primitive armor and weapons, however he is rigidly trained and his equipment does not slow him down. They are usually fanatical to the Cult of Mars and are made up of both slaves and born-into-slavery, exclusively only taken from male stock. They are the common front-line troop of The Legion and come with a variety of loadouts to suit niches! Veteran Legionaries are more adept warriors of the Legion whom have been provided better armor and some command power over their recruit brethren.

Followers: ​Mostly composed of those born into the Legion or of entrusted captures thoroughly broken of any will. They are considered people of the Legion and generally are treated more fairly than their lowery slaves. They still however are absolutely near the bottom of the chain and commonly still see harassment from Legionnaires and can expect extremely harsh punishment for failures.

Slaves​: The lowest rung of the Legion. Slaves are little more than captures who have proven their worth enough to be considered a Slave. They have no rights and are as disposable as they come. Do not expect to live long if you’ve a sharp tongue as a slave!

Capture:​Anyone whom the Legion captures is brought in as this ‘cleverly’ named title. They have to prove themselves worthy enough to be considered a slave or else face death.

Captains: ​The commander of the garrison and armed NCR troops in the area! This is the highest rank you can currently achieve within the game without any admin intervention ICly. Captains are trained personnel who’ve gained multiple tours of experience and have fought to the bone with skillful tactics to earn their pins. The troopers under their command should have respect for them, for they’ve bled the same they have! You are the highest of the currently acquirable roles in the NCR! You are conditioned and generally have some form of respect for the republic. You shouldn’t purposefully try to commit war-crimes against the NCR regulations! (Ford D. Mongers need not apply!)

Veteran Ranger: ​A resilient- legendary elite sect of NCR Rangers who are the best, of the best, of those best! Their leadership typically differs from the usual command structure, but can still

take orders from Captains, and Ambassadors. They’re served a minimum of 20 years of service! (So they can’t be very young!) They command the Rangers deployed within Pahrump and Sunnydale in cohesion with the Captain.

Rangers: ​A elite special forces group of the New California Republic, who are widely known for their reconnaissance, and being capable to deal with issues regular members of the NCRA wouldn’t be able to handle regularly. They take orders from the highest CO currently active, otherwise- take orders exclusively from the Veteran Ranger.

Ambassadors: ​A diplomatic envoy for the regions they travel to, their statesmanship unrivaled by those around them, they ensure the New California Republic is properly represented, and further their edicts and agendas accordingly. Although not a martial leader, they’re more of a political figure, but that doesn’t mean they can’t use their governmental might to see change or make orders in the garrison!

Lieutenant: ​The second in command before the Captain, the lowest ranking officer that can be found in Pahrump and Sunnydale, assisting the Captain, or in the place of one, managing and supervising the NCRA and the operations undertaken in Sunnydale and Pahrump.

Sergeants: ​The highest ranking regular uniform soldier present in Pahrump and Sunnydale, typically the ones that take control of squads that are organized- and being a proud example of the NCRA to the newer enlistments!

Heavy Troopers: ​A specially fitted soldier with exclusive access and training with power-armor,. Albeit with the servos and pistons removed. These soldiers are typically the ones that will take the head on the frontline and defend their fellow trooper in the name of the republic! They take orders like every other soldier, but are special in their own right due to their unique armor, you are much more trained than the usual soldier, and better equipped.

Specialists: ​A trooper with glorified equipment, you get better training and discipline then the usual trooper, you’re a peg up from the meatshield- a ​glorified meatshield!​You’re much more qualified than the usual NCR Trooper.

Troopers: ​The common, backbone soldier that can be found in the ranks, brought into the army after two to three weeks of training to be prepared for the harshness of the wasteland, quickly trained to push to the frontlines as soon as possible!

Military Police: ​Military Police are the corrective personnel of the NCRA, they ensure that the NCR garrisons of Sunnydale and Pahrump follow their laws properly. Abandoning the garrison without official reasoning will be subject to either desertion, or abandoning their post!

Non-Combatants: ​Civilians from the NCR who for one reason or another, find themselves amongst the army garrisons. This may be for scoping enlistment, on behalf of the press, or

other grounds! Typically armed with a lowly handgun for their self-defense, they shouldn’t want to typically partake in combat, unless in self-defense or in defense of others.

Brotherhood of Steel

Chain of Command of BoS

-Elder

-Head Scribe/Senior Paladin

-Paladin

-Scribe/Knight

Elder​: The Eldest of a Chapters ranks. The Elder is the De-Facto leader of their given Chapter and decision makers amongst their ranks. Known to dictate even the smallest delegations inside their fortresses. Regardless of their history as either apart of the Military or Science divisions of the Brotherhood, they’ve absolute control over both branches.

Head-Scribe:​The Leader of the Scientific branch of the Brotherhood of Steel. They delegate the recovery and use of technology from the wasteland within the Chapter. They’re often the next replacement for the Elder if they fall due to being of highly intelligent backgrounds- Though

some chapters prefer to promote their Elders from Paladin stock.

Senior Paladin:​The Leader of the Militaristic branch in the Chapter. Paladin was originally a rank given to those of the Brotherhood's primary combat force. However, the local chapter’s low-numbers has dictated that the Paladin is the best equipped member of the Brotherhood's security detail. Originally only Paladins were the only ones provided power-armor, though this restriction has been lax a hundred years after the Brotherhood walked out of Mariposa.

Paladin:​Entrusted with the Venerable but sturdy T-45 power armor, Paladins are the security detail for their Chapter. Using the finest technology and weapons to obtain a tactical edge over anyone who would counteract the Brotherhood's goals.

Knight:​Primarily composed of Initiates who wished to get into some manner of tinkering, the Knights maintain the bunker and all material used by the Chapter. They're more-or-less glorified Engineers, but no less important.

Scribe:​Scientists, technologically savvy and competently raised with pre-war tech. Some of the brightest minds in the wasteland have come from Scribe-Stock.

Initiate:​The Youngest of the Chapter. They’ve yet to be trained either as a Scribe or Knight and thus are equipped without the power-armor the faction is synonymous for!

Regulations of the Brotherhood of Steel

-Abide by the Chains that Bind! ​A typical member of this chapter was born and raised in it! Conditioned from birth to respect the order of command, you will function as the Chains that Bind dictates!

-Respect your brothers and sisters! ​You’ve known them for years!

-Do not compromise the chapter! ​Actions such as publicly stating your presence, intentional or not, runs a risk to the Brotherhood of Steel!

-Remember the bear rivalry! ​They’ve wronged your people in the past, the NCR should be heavily disliked, and alliances with them should be for very good reasons to preserve the chapter!

-Remain hidden! ​The Sunnydale/Pahrump chapters of the Brotherhood of Steel are exceptionally inclusive and secretive!

-Retrieve technology! ​Preservation of dangerous technology can only be handled by the Brotherhood of Steel! The wasters cannot be trusted with dangerous tech.

-Tread with caution! ​Every little step can lead to the discovery of a member of the chapter- or worse, the bunker! Usage of the vertibird should be only for good reasons, and disguises should be commonly made advantage of!
